## Career and background

I have 20+ years of experience in the IT industry. My career took me to wear the shoes in varied roles, from IT architect, project management, consulting, engineering, and sales, both in individual contributor and leadership roles. I have worked and lived in multiple countries in Latin America and Europe. I now live in Germany with my family, and we are expecting our new baby in July, just after the module finishes.

I started young as a co-founder of Nebula, where we developed and maintained a Linux security-appliance distro for the SOHO segment at an affordable price point. I then moved to work for large industry leaders being later in IBM, VMware and most recently Broadcom. My main technical domains are cyber-security, cyber-resiliency, cloud and infrastructure in general. I am currently on paternity leave to take care of our first little son while we await the new one. In my last role I was in charge of the Cloud portfolio for VMware across several European countries, being responsible for the offering itself, the zones and PoDs used in different hyperscalers delivery to clients, the engineering and sales teams, with revenue objectives over the portfolio.

## Technical depth and hands-on practice

My recent leadership roles have not been engineering hands-on, but I remain deeply technical by nature. I still join architecture reviews and get involved in customer crit sits, and sometimes find myself discussing a bug fix with developers, not only managing the escalation. At home, my basement still has a few servers running dozens of Docker images.

## Qualifications and certifications

I hold several technical and professional certifications, including IBM IT Architect, Red Hat Certified Engineer, and Architect, Microsoft Certified Engineer and Architect, amongst many others. Before starting this MSc AI, I went through building some formal foundations beyond my education years and professional experience, completing the Andrew Ng and Stanford Machine Learning Specialization and the Duke Data Science Math Skills course in Coursera.

## Motivation for the MSc AI

I'm a witness in the front line of how machine learning and AI are foundational technologies already changing entire industries. Although I have been exposed to ML, I want to understand deeper, not only as an occasional practitioner or non-hands-on leader, I want to understand deep and be fully hands-on. This MSc AI is one of many actions towards it, in this case the formal one to build that capacity. I expect to contribute even more in the near future with changing roles for an AI-focused one where I can help to shape how AI/ML technologies contribute.
